I've been working on this broadcast message system I'm testing with a local dance studio (I'll be opening the service to the public after it's tested). While working on this, I realized that the recording/broadcast pieces in Twilio are completely separate from either other ... A person can call, record a message, and have it broadcast to hundreds of people without having to wait for all the calls to go through.

I thought about this aspect of broadcast messages a bit, and I think I see a way to make a global "Chinese Whispers" or "Telephone" type game...

The game begins like this:

  1. The first person calls the Twilio number
  2. The app has you enter the phone number of the next person to play the game
  3. You have 10 (?) seconds to tell part of a story
  4. The phone number you entered is dialed after your time is up

The story continues (forever?) like this (the order can probably be modified a bit):

  1. A person receives the phone call from this app
  2. The app has them enter the phone number of the next person to play the game
  3. The previous person's 10 (?) second clip is played
  4. This person's 10 second story clip is recorded
  5. The app immediately sends off the call to the number in the background
  6. This person's call continues, playing the story over from the very beginning
